teaching

Previously, I have taught Physics 136, the laboratory cognate for Introductory Physics for Life Scientists. The laboratory section is intended to enhance the students' understanding of basic physics concepts that they are taught in lecture by allowing them hands-on experience and the opportunity to interact more closely with an instructor. In the laboratory section, we cover a variety of topics, ranging from uniform acceleration, to pulleys, to heating and cooling. The mathematics used in Physics 136 has a heavy tendancy towards statistics to better suit the career trajectories of the life science students, as opposed to the heavy calculus focus of other introductory physics classes.

Outside of the formal classroom, I have also been able to involve myself with the University of Michigan chapter of Society of Women in Physics (SWIP) which is involved with various outreach projects. These outreach projects have provided me the opportunity to teach to younger and more impressionable students than the college students in my lab classes, which is often more challenging and much more energy intensive.
